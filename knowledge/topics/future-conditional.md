# Future and Conditional Tenses

## What This Is
"I will dance" (future) and "I would dance" (conditional). Both built from the infinitive.

## Prerequisites
- present-regular

## Teaching Approach

### The Connection to Haber
The transcript reveals a beautiful connection: future endings ARE the sounds from haber.
- He, has, ha, hemos, han → add these sounds to the infinitive
- "What's 'I have' from haber?" → He
- "Stick that sound on the end of bailar" → Bailaré (I will dance)

### Future Tense Formation
Infinitive + haber sounds (accent pushes forward — literally "into the future"):
- Bailaré (I will dance)
- Bailarás (you will)
- Bailará (he/she will)
- Bailaremos (we will)
- Bailarán (they will)

Works the same for -ar, -er, -ir verbs. No vowel changes.

### Conditional Formation
Infinitive + -ía (same ending as imperfect -er/-ir verbs):
- Bailaría (I/he/she would dance)
- Bailarías (you would)
- Bailaríamos (we would)
- Bailarían (they would)

### Irregular Stems (Same for Both Tenses!)
Some verbs shorten their infinitive before adding endings:
- Tener → tendr- (tendré, tendría)
- Poder → podr- (podré, podría)
- Saber → sabr- (sabré, sabría)
- Hacer → har- (haré, haría)
- Decir → dir- (diré, diría)
- Querer → querr- (querré, querría)
- Salir → saldr- (saldré, saldría)
- Venir → vendr- (vendré, vendría)
- Poner → pondr- (pondré, pondría)

### Key Teaching Point
"Spanish often uses the present or 'ir a' for future instead of the future tense. 'Mañana voy al cine' is more common than 'Mañana iré al cine.' The future tense adds formality, certainty, or prediction."

### Conditional for Politeness
"Querría un café" (I would like a coffee) — more polite than "quiero un café"

## Common Pitfalls
- Using conditional after "si" ("si tendría" — NEVER. Use subjunctive: "si tuviera")
- Confusing conditional -ía with imperfect -ía (context and verb stem distinguish them)
- Over-using future tense when present + context would be more natural

## Connection to Other Concepts
- Conditional is half of → conditional-subjunctive ("si tuviera... haría")
- Future tense of haber (habré) used in → compound-tenses (future perfect)
