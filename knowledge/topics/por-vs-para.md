# Por vs. Para

## What This Is
Two words for "for" with distinct uses. Another classic intermediate challenge.

## Prerequisites
- prepositions

## Teaching Approach

### The Core Framework
**Para** = destination/purpose (where it's going, what it's for)
**Por** = cause/exchange/through (why, because of, in exchange for)

"Para looks forward — purpose, goal, deadline. Por looks backward — reason, cause, motivation."

### Para Uses (Purpose / Destination / Deadline)
1. **Purpose**: ¿Para qué? (What for?) — Estudio para aprender. (I study to learn.)
2. **Recipient**: Es para ti. (It's for you.)
3. **Destination**: Salgo para Madrid. (I leave for Madrid.)
4. **Deadline**: Es para mañana. (It's for/due tomorrow.)
5. **Opinion**: Para mí, es difícil. (For me / In my opinion, it's difficult.)
6. **Comparison**: Para un niño, habla bien. (For a child, he speaks well.)

### Por Uses (Cause / Exchange / Through)
1. **Cause/Reason**: ¿Por qué? (Why? / Because of what?) — Por eso. (Because of that.)
2. **Exchange**: Gracias por todo. (Thanks for everything.) — Lo compré por 20 euros.
3. **Duration**: Viví allí por tres años. (I lived there for three years.)
4. **Through/Along**: Caminé por el parque. (I walked through the park.)
5. **Means**: Por teléfono. (By phone.) Por correo. (By mail.)
6. **On behalf of**: Lo hice por ti. (I did it for you / because of you.)
7. **Per**: Tres veces por semana. (Three times per week.)
8. **Passive agent**: Escrito por Cervantes. (Written by Cervantes.)

### The Contrast That Clarifies Everything
"Lo hice **para** ti" = I made it FOR you (you're the recipient — here, take it)
"Lo hice **por** ti" = I did it BECAUSE OF you (you're the reason — you inspired/caused it)

### Mnemonics
- **Para** = PARpose, PARticipant (who receives, what it's for)
- **Por** = PORque (because), exchange

## Common Pitfalls
- Using para for duration ("para tres años" — wrong, use por)
- Using por for deadlines ("por mañana" — wrong, use para)
- "Gracias para" instead of "Gracias por" (thanks is about cause, not purpose)
- The "lo hice para ti" vs "lo hice por ti" confusion

## Connection to Other Concepts
- Por qué (why) vs porque (because) vs para que (so that + subjunctive)
- "Para que" triggers → subjunctive-triggers
