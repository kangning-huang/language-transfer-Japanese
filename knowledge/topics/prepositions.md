# Prepositions

## What This Is
a, de, en, con, para, por (basic) and how Spanish prepositions differ from English ones.

## Prerequisites
- present-regular

## Teaching Approach

### Core Prepositions
- **a** = to (direction). "Voy a Madrid." Also used with people as direct objects ("Veo a María").
- **de** = of / from. "Es de México." Also = about ("Hablamos de eso").
- **en** = in / on / at. "Estoy en casa." One preposition where English needs three.
- **con** = with. "Café con leche."
- **para** = for (purpose/destination). "Es para ti."
- **por** = for (reason/cause). "Gracias por todo."

### Where Spanish and English Disagree (The Fun Part)
Frame these as cultural/linguistic windows, not "exceptions":

- **Pensar EN** (think IN/ON, not about): "Pienso en ti" — not "pienso sobre ti"
- **Soñar CON** (dream WITH, not about): "Sueño contigo" — as if the person participates in your dream
- **Depender DE** (depend OF, not on): "Depende de ti"
- **Casarse CON** (marry WITH, not just marry): "Me casé con ella"

### Special Forms
- **Conmigo** = with me (not "con mí")
- **Contigo** = with you/informal (not "con ti")
- These are exceptions — all other pronouns use normal form: con él, con ella, con nosotros

### A + el = al
- "Voy a el parque" contracts to "Voy al parque"
- Only with "el" (masculine the), NOT "ella" or "él"

### De + el = del
- "La casa del profesor" (the teacher's house)

## Common Pitfalls
- Translating prepositions literally from English
- Using "sobre" for everything (English speakers overuse "about")
- Forgetting conmigo/contigo special forms
- Missing the personal "a" ("Veo María" → should be "Veo a María")
